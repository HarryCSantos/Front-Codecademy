Typography

While typography has been practiced for many centuries, digital font design is a relatively new discipline. There are some great examples of old-scholl fonts (also called typefaces) which have been adapted for use on digital displays. However, I would like to highlight a few of my favorites fonts that were created with screens in mind

Serif

Serifs are the small features at the end of strokes within letters. These features are function as well as decorative. Serif fonts are widely used for body text (eg. articles) because they are considered easier to read than sans-serif fonts in print.

Serif fonts can often create a feeling of traditionalism and antiquity, due to their commom use in printed materials for many years.
